Secure Hydro through Advanced Manufacturing

Modernizing the U.S. hydropower fleet with AMM

DOE - ORNL | Advanced Manufacturing and Materials

The Aging Infrastructure Challenge

  • Hydropower delivers 30% of U.S. renewable generation
  • Provides 93% of grid-scale energy storage
  • Most units were built in the 1940s to 1960s
  • Forced outage hours have trended upward since 2010

Supply Chain Risks

  • Three manufacturers control ~75% of turbine capacity
  • Legacy parts and dies are no longer available
  • Reverse engineering and custom machining raise costs
  • DOE flags casting and forging gaps as security risks

Advanced Manufacturing and Materials

  • Digital design + novel materials + new fabrication
  • Shorter lead times with on-demand production
  • Domestic manufacturing for critical large parts
  • Higher performance with lighter, stronger components
  • Obsolete parts solved through scanning and printing

Pillar 1 - Additive Manufacturing

  • DED for large metal parts like runners and wicket gates
  • PBF for high-resolution components with complex channels
  • Large-format polymer printing for sand-cast tooling
  • Concrete printing enables on-site civil structures

Pillar 2 - Innovative Materials

  • FRPCs are lighter and corrosion resistant
  • Potential for decades of added component life
  • Functionally graded materials merge ductile and hard zones
  • Targets cavitation and erosion on turbine surfaces

Pillar 3 - Advanced Repair and Coatings

  • Cold spray deposits metal without melting the base
  • Repairs show 3x cavitation resistance vs. fillers
  • Self-healing and anti-fouling coatings reduce downtime
  • Environmentally acceptable lubricants reduce risk

Case Study - Nimbus Dam

  • Legacy log boom anchor no longer manufactured
  • Redesigned for PBF printing and part consolidation
  • Mass reduced by ~50% with equivalent performance
  • Cost decreased versus traditional fabrication

Case Study - Emrgy Inc.

  • Hydrofoil tooling costs were prohibitive
  • Large-format polymer AM for sand-cast patterns
  • 78% tooling cost savings
  • Enabled corrosion-resistant alloy adoption

Unlocking Non-Powered Dams

  • Only ~3% of U.S. dams generate power
  • Potential for 4.8 GW by 2050
  • High LCOE blocks small projects
  • AMM enables modular turbines and printed civil works

Future Flexibility and Environment

  • PSH benefits from advanced membranes and magnets
  • Complex blade geometries improve fish survival
  • Design freedom reduces ecological impact

Ecosystem Approach

  • Workforce development is essential
  • Shared data reduces perceived technical risk
  • Standards (ASTM, ISO) enable broader adoption

Triple-Helix Partnership

  • Academia and labs: fundamental research (e.g., ORNL)
  • Industry: validation and pilot deployments
  • Government: funding high-risk R and D

Mapping Solutions to Challenges

  • Legacy parts - scanning and AM replacement
  • Foreign supply - domestic DED and hybrid manufacturing
  • O and M costs - cold spray repair and sensors
  • Growth - modular designs and on-site printing

Result: more resilient, lower-cost hydropower.
