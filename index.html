What is Hydrokinetic Energy?

  • Definition: Energy derived from the motion of water (kinetic energy) rather than its potential energy (head).
  • Analogy: "Underwater Wind Turbines" - utilizing water density instead of air.
  • Key Difference:
    • Conventional Hydro: Uses potential energy (Dam height).
    • Hydrokinetic: Uses kinetic energy (Flow velocity).
Group 10

Historical Context

  • Ancient Origins: Water wheels (M.Ö. 4000) used for grinding grain were the first use of kinetic water energy.
  • Modern Evolution (1920s): Georges Darrieus proposed vertical axis turbines for wind, which was later adapted for water.
  • Current Era: Combining ancient principles with modern aerodynamics and hydrodynamics (submarine tech).

Working Principle

Power generation is based on the kinetic energy equation:

$$ P = \frac{1}{2} \rho A v^3 C_p $$
  • $P$: Power output (Watts)
  • $\rho$: Water density ($\approx 1000 \text{ kg/m}^3$, 830x denser than air)
  • $A$: Swept area ($m^2$)
  • $v$: Flow velocity ($m/s$) - Crucial Factor ($v^3$)
  • $C_p$: Power coefficient

Hydrokinetic Energy in Turkey

Turkey has significant potential in straits and irrigation channels.

Key Projects & Initiatives
  • Mavi İda Enerji (Çanakkale): Developing the "M30" domestic current turbine for the Bosphorus/Dardanelles.
  • Adana Irrigation Channels: Pilot projects using portable turbines in DSİ channels (ECC Makina).
  • TOBB ETÜ (Ankara): Developing "Container Type HPP" and in-pipe turbines for municipal water lines (İZSU).
Group 10

Potential & Challenges in Turkey

Potential

  • The Straits (Bosphorus): A natural energy corridor with continuous two-way currents.
  • Rivers: Euphrates (Fırat) and Tigris (Dicle).
  • Irrigation: GAP region channels.

Challenges

  • Maritime Traffic: Busy shipping lanes in Bosphorus.
  • Anchoring: Difficult seabed conditions.
  • Maintenance: Biofouling and underwater access.

Civil Engineering Aspects

  • Site Selection: Bathymetry mapping and ADCP velocity surveys.
  • Foundation Design:
    • Gravity Base (Concrete weight)
    • Monopiles (Driven steel piles)
    • Mooring Systems (Anchors & Cables)
  • Grid Connection: Subsea cabling and onshore substations.

Summary

  • Hydrokinetic energy is a sustainable alternative to dams, utilizing water velocity ($P \propto v^3$).
  • Offers high energy density and predictability.
  • Turkey's Role: Significant potential in Bosphorus and irrigation channels with ongoing R&D (Mavi İda, TOBB ETÜ).
  • Civil engineers are vital for infrastructure, foundation, and deployment.
