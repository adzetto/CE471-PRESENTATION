Engineering Hydrokinetic Power

Resource assessment, AMM, and deployment

Hydrokinetic systems without dams

Hydrokinetic vs Conventional Hydro

  • Conventional hydro relies on head from dams
  • Hydrokinetic uses moving water energy directly
  • Flows from rivers, tides, and ocean currents
  • No impoundments or large reservoirs required

Resource Assessment

  • Current velocity typically greater than 1.5 m/s
  • Predictable tidal cycles or stable river flow
  • Suitable depth and seabed conditions
  • Examples: Salish Sea, Pentland Firth, Bosphorus

Case Study - Bosphorus Strait

  • Two-layer flow driven by elevation and salinity
  • Upper layer flows south, lower layer flows north
  • Surface currents often exceed 2.0 m/s
  • Flow varies with wind and Danube inflow

Engineering the Conversion System

  • Turbine runner and blades capture kinetic energy
  • Gearbox and generator form the powertrain
  • Gravity-base or moored structural supports
  • Casting and forging are slow and expensive

A New Manufacturing Paradigm

  • LCOE = (CAPEX + OPEX) / Annual Energy Production
  • AMM reduces CAPEX and OPEX
  • Design freedom increases energy production
  • Faster iterations shorten deployment cycles

Metal Additive Manufacturing

  • PBF: high resolution, complex internal channels
  • DED: large volumes, ideal for major components
  • Enables rapid repair and part replacement
  • Reduces reliance on large castings

Composites and Printed Concrete

  • FRPC wicket gates add ~35 years of life
  • Composite penstocks reduce corrosion risk
  • Printed concrete enables on-site foundations
  • Logistics costs drop for remote sites

AMM in Practice

  • Nimbus Dam: 50% mass reduction with AM redesign
  • Emrgy Inc.: 78% tooling cost savings
  • Rapid prototyping for hydrofoils and spokes

Act III - The MeyGen Project

  • Pentland Firth tidal energy project in Scotland
  • Phase 1 demo: 6 MW operational since 2018
  • Lease potential up to 398 MW
  • Over 37 GWh generated by 2020

MeyGen Turbine Details

  • Atlantis AR1500: 1.5 MW, 18 m rotor, yawed nacelle
  • Andritz HS1500: 1.5 MW with active blade monitoring
  • Pitch control optimizes power across tidal cycles

Project Stroma Subsea Hub

  • Connects multiple turbines to one export cable
  • Reduces drilling, vessel time, and costs
  • Simplifies expansion for future arrays

Environmental Monitoring

  • Active acoustics, cameras, and tagging used
  • Marine life avoids rotors during operation
  • No collisions observed in monitoring campaigns

Act IV - Optimization via Cold Spray

  • Supersonic deposition repairs cavitation damage
  • 3x higher cavitation resistance than fillers
  • Extends service life with minimal downtime

Functionally Graded Materials

  • Transition from ductile core to hard surface
  • Improves cavitation and erosion resistance
  • Eliminates coating de-bonding risk

Digital Twins and Smart Components

  • Embedded sensors printed during fabrication
  • Real-time monitoring for predictive maintenance
  • Improves reliability and planning

Unlocking Non-Powered Dams

  • Over 90,000 U.S. dams with no generation
  • Modular designs reduce CAPEX for small sites
  • Lower-cost materials improve project viability

Collaborative Ecosystem

  • University, industry, and government partnership
  • Moves TRL from concept to deployment
  • Shared infrastructure reduces risk

Non-Technical Needs

  • Workforce training for new manufacturing methods
  • Open data to build confidence in reliability
  • Testing facilities for late-stage validation

Conclusion

  • Hydrokinetic power bridges water engineering and AMM
  • Resource assessment guides viable sites
  • Advanced materials unlock performance and durability
  • Deployment success needs technical and social systems
